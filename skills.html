Which Skills do your Students' Need to Practice?

The first step to create a useful and successful literature-based learning unit, is to determine which skills you want your students to practice throughout the unit. You can create a wonderful unit, but what is the use if your students are not gaining the necessary skills they need at this time.
Below you will find a list of common skills by age that you may wish to focus on during your unit.
You'll notice that many skills are listed under more than one age group. Children develop and gain skills in a predicable pattern and in their own timing so some children may be working on certain skills sooner or later than we expect.
Note This list is not exhaustive, but merely a jumping off point to help you starting thinking about skills your students may be working on too.

Toddlers

  • Fine motor
  • Gross Motor
  • Basics of Play
  • Sensory Exploration
  • Basic Hygiene
  • Toiletting
  • Nature Exploration

3 Year Olds

  • Fine Motor
  • Gross Motor
  • Basic Hygiene
  • Toiletting
  • Appropriately entering play
  • Recognitizing signs of the season

4 Year Olds

  • Pincer Grasp
  • Kindness
  • Increase Vocabulary
  • Rhyming
  • Letter Recognition
  • Labelling Emotions
  • Telling vs. Asking
  • Facts and Non-fiction
