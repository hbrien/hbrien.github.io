How to Evaluate A Book

It can be intimidating to pick one book for a whole unit.

Once you have in mind the skills want you want your students to work on and clear idea of their interests it becomes easier to narrow down which book would be a good jumping off point.

The best books to base a unit around are multi-faceted . They speak to the main skill you want to practice, and they include other topics sprinkled that you can pull from as well. After reading the story to the students you can see which parts are most interesting to them as we read and bring more projects on that topic to our classroom.

Take a look at some of the books that you already have with fresh eyes to see how many topics you can pull from one book. If you can see 4 or more topics , chances are it's a good candidate for a unit.

Here's a list of some great choices for a unit.

  • One of this author's favorite examples is Stick and Stone by Beth Ferry

    This book covers themes of friendship and kindness and it also includes number sense, physics, nature exploration and more!
  • Dinosaur Bones by Bob Barner
  • Katy and the Big Snow by Virginia Lee Burton
  • Pumpkin Soup by Helen Cooper
  • Room on the Broom by Julia Donaldson
  • Dream You'll Be by Joseph T. Garcia
  • Moon's First Friend by Susanna Leonard Hill
  • I Will Never Not Ever Eat a Tomato by Lauren Child
  • Our Table by Peter Reynolds
  • The Hungry Caterpillar by Eric Carl
  • Baby Bear Sees Blue by Ashley Wolff
  • Snowmen At Night by Caralyn Buehner
  • Tomatoes for Neela by Padma Lakshmi
  • Lola Plants a Garden by Anna McQuinn

...these are just a few examples of stories that would make great choices for a learning unit! See what else you can discover.
